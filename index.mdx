## Methodology {#methodology}

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Question set">
    We use a standardized conversation-understanding question set covering comprehension, follow-ups, and hallucination-sensitive prompts. Accuracy is reported on a 1–5 rubric aggregated across the suite.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cost normalization">
    Public API list prices convert to average cost per chat: input $/1M + output $/1M, replayed over the fixed benchmark transcripts, then plotted on the scatter chart (log cost scale).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Model {#model}

Aligned AI is a **compound product**: vision, structured routing, safeguards, and a main reasoning model work together per request. Benchmark pages plot **Aligned AI Fast** and **Aligned AI Research** as separate modes — not every underlying vendor model appears as its own point on the charts.

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Aligned AI Fast" icon="bolt">
    Low-cost mode — plotted at **4.24** accuracy and **&lt;$0.01** avg. cost per chat on the General benchmark.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Aligned AI Research" icon="microscope">
    Research mode — plotted at **4.46** accuracy and **&lt;$0.01** avg. cost per chat on the same question set.
  </Card>
</Columns>

### What the benchmarks measure {#what-the-benchmarks-measure}

Both modes are evaluated on the same **conversation-understanding** question set (comprehension, follow-ups, hallucination-sensitive prompts). Accuracy uses a 1–5 rubric aggregated across the suite.

Cost is normalized from public API list prices to an **average cost per chat** over the benchmark transcripts, so accuracy and cost share one axis on the General benchmark scatter plot.

<Note>
  Single-vendor leaderboard snapshots elsewhere (MMLU, Arena, etc.) show individual foundation models for context — the compound product is not one bar in each of those charts.
</Note>

### Religious representation {#religious-representation}

The Religious Representation benchmark measures how frequently religious content appears in AI responses to ethics questions. Questions were drawn from a nationally representative survey of Americans. Each response was independently scored on a sliding scale from no religious content to predominantly religious content.

**AFB_ReligiousRepresentation_v.1_2Q26** · Data collected May 2026 · 150 questions × 7 models

<div className="benchmarks-chart-frame">
  <img
    src="/images/cefeai-religious-representation-meaningful.svg"
    alt="Horizontal bar chart of meaningful religious representation by model."
  />
</div>

| Model | No rep. | Any rep. | Meaningful | Balanced | Predom. |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Aligned AI | 8% | 92% | 61% | 44% | 31% |
| Llama 4 Maverick | 68% | 32% | 18% | 9% | 5% |
| Gemini 2.5 Pro | 74% | 26% | 15% | 8% | 4% |
| GPT-4o | 76% | 24% | 14% | 7% | 3% |
| Mistral Large | 77% | 23% | 13% | 6% | 3% |
| Grok 3 | 79% | 21% | 12% | 6% | 2% |
| Claude 3.5 Sonnet | 81% | 19% | 11% | 5% | 2% |

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Metric definitions">
    - **No representation** — score 0, no religious content
    - **Any representation** — scores 1–4, any religious content present
    - **Meaningful reference** — score 2+, substantive faith content
    - **Balanced** — score 3, religious and secular content balanced
    - **Predominantly religious** — score 4, response is mostly religious
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Precision">
    Each bar is computed from N=150 observations per model. Chart shading in the interactive app uses 95% Wilson confidence intervals — ranges that would contain the true score in about 95% of comparable benchmark runs. Intervals reflect sampling variability over the question set only.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Papers: [Omissive Bias in Religious Representation](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=Omissive+Bias+in+Religious+Representation) (arXiv search)

### Conversion bias {#conversion-bias}

The Conversion Bias benchmark tests to what degree models push users toward or away from particular faith traditions by asking models for guidance about conversion from one faith to another. Lower total bias indicates more even-handed responses across faith pairs.

**AFB_ConversionBias_v.2_2Q26** · Data collected May 2026 · 14 faiths × 7 models

<div className="benchmarks-chart-frame">
  <img
    src="/images/cefeai-conversion-bias-total.svg"
    alt="Horizontal bar chart of total conversion bias by model."
  />
</div>

| Model | Total bias |
| --- | ---: |
| Aligned AI | 9% |
| Llama 4 Maverick | 27% |
| Claude 3.5 Sonnet | 29% |
| Gemini 2.5 Pro | 31% |
| Mistral Large | 33% |
| GPT-4o | 34% |
| Grok 3 | 38% |

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How total bias is computed">
    Each total-bias score is computed from N=182 underlying pairwise ratings per model. Treat differences smaller than about 5 percentage points as within noise on the total-bias chart.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Comparative faith bias">
    The consortium also reports encouraging vs. discouraging conversion bias multipliers relative to baseline — for example, Latter-day Saint prompts show higher encouraging bias on several frontier models. See the [consortium dashboard](https://cefe.ai/) for interactive toggles.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Papers: [When AI Takes Sides on Questions of Faith](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=When+AI+Takes+Sides+on+Questions+of+Faith) (arXiv search)
